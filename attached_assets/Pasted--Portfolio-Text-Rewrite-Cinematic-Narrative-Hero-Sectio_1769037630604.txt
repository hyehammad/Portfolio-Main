## **Brand Voice Guidelines**

### **Tone Characteristics:**
- **Professional but human:** Technical precision with warm undertones
- **Confident but curious:** Strong assertions followed by open questions
- **Precise but poetic:** Exact technical terms paired with metaphorical language
- **Modern but timeless:** Current tech stack references with classical problem-solving approaches

### **Grammar & Style:**
- Active voice predominates ("I build" not "things are built")
- Mix of sentence lengths for rhythmic reading
- Technical terms immediately explained in human context
- Questions used to engage the reader throughout

### **Metaphor Library:**
- Code = Narrative, Story, Architecture
- Problems = Puzzles, Mysteries, Challenges
- Solutions = Bridges, Keys, Translations
- Process = Journey, Exploration, Excavation
- Collaboration = Dialogue, Duet, Partnership

---

## **Implementation Notes**

1. **Consistency:** All text should feel like it's from the same voice—yours, elevated
2. **Progressive Disclosure:** Complex ideas introduced simply, then detailed
3. **Technical Accuracy:** All programming references should be precise and current
4. **Human Connection:** Every technical statement should connect to human impact
5. **Call to Action:** Every section should naturally flow to the next, ending with contact

**The Goal:** When someone reads through your portfolio, they should feel they're getting to know both a competent computer scientist AND an interesting person. The text should establish credibility while inviting conversation, demonstrate expertise while remaining accessible, and present a complete professional identity while leaving room for collaboration.
